Situated on the campus of the University of California, Burkley, the Essig Museam of Entomology is a research effort dedicated to the study and preservation of arthropods. They not only study the creatures, but also encourage preservation efforts and house a variety of programs that encourage the citizens of California to help their cause, and learn more about the world around us. They provided the endangered list of which this site based its selection upon, and overall are a wonderful resource for those interested in the biodiversity of North America. Their site is Essig.berekeley.edu

A nonprofit specializing in the conservation and research of California's endangered arthropod population, the Xerces Society for Invertabrate Conservation is dedicated to being both a trusted source of accurate information and direct participant in restorative projects. They work with many communities and professionals to maintain healthy populations and restore threatened species to their formal glory, hoping to challenge negative effects humans have on these insects in order to save them from extinction. Their website is Xerces.org

The California Department of Fish and Wildlife, though not exclusivley focused on arthropods, is a state agency responsible for the official preservation and protection of California's wildlife and habitats. It hosts a wide library of recources of endangered species and any conservation efforts regarding them, and is directly responsible for most state-sanctioned actions and laws regarding the safty of these critters. Their website is wildlide.gov
